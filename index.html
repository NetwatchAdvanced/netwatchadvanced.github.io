03

DNS Layer — Strongly Recommended

Recursive DNS Deployment

▾

DNS is the address book of the internet — every connection your devices make starts with a DNS query. Deploying a local recursive resolver means no third-party DNS provider sees your queries, every device on your network is covered whether it wants to be or not, and known-bad domains are blocked before a connection is ever attempted. This tier is a meaningful standalone deployment — and it feeds the NetWatch dashboard with DNS telemetry when Tier 4 follows.

Pi-hole network-wide ad & tracker blocking
Unbound fully recursive resolver
DNSSEC validation
Forced DNS routing — no device bypass
Query logging & analytics
Custom blocklist configuration
Note: Tier 4 (NetWatch) can be deployed without a DNS layer, but the picture it gives you is less complete. DNS telemetry is one of the most revealing data sources in the dashboard — it's what tells you an Echo Dot tried to bypass your resolver 40 times today. We recommend Tier 3 first, but won't force it.

04

Full Deployment — The NetWatch Platform

Heuristic & Signature IDS Monitoring

▾

NetWatch is a purpose-built detection platform — not a prevention system. It passively watches every packet via SPAN port mirroring without sitting inline, meaning it generates no performance impact and cannot intercept, block, or modify traffic. What it does is give you complete visibility into what your network is actually doing: every connection, every external IP, every anomalous behavior pattern — unified in a single operational dashboard alongside your DNS telemetry. You see everything. You own the hardware. The data never leaves your network.

Suricata IDS — signature & heuristic detection
Passive SPAN port monitoring (no inline impact)
Real-time alert dashboard
Geographic traffic intelligence
Device inventory & discovery engine
DNS telemetry integration
Monthly reports — Passive or Active tier
Client-owned hardware, full data sovereignty
Detection, not prevention. NetWatch tells you what's happening — it does not stop it. That's an honest limitation and an important one to understand before deployment. The value is visibility: knowing a device is behaving abnormally, that traffic is going somewhere it shouldn't, that something needs your attention. What happens next is a conversation. Operator-assisted remote response is on the roadmap for clients who want a human in the loop when an alert fires.
