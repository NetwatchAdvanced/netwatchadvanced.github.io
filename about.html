How this started

A decade in the field.
I know what I saw.

After spending a decade in the beverage industry covering multiple states in the Southern US and working directly with independent businesses, I saw the same network problems everywhere. POS terminals sharing infrastructure with guest WiFi. No segmentation between staff devices and back-of-house systems. No visibility into what was connected or what it was doing. No one to call when something went wrong. The businesses I worked with were running the same risks as enterprise clients without any of the resources or support those clients take for granted.

That experience gave me a clear picture of the gap: independent businesses need professional IT support just as much as larger clients, but the providers built to deliver it aren't built for them. MSPs have minimums. Enterprise tools have enterprise price tags. The result is that a bar, a restaurant, or a law office ends up with consumer-grade gear and no real support structure. That's what this is built to address.

NetWatch Advanced is not just a monitoring product. It's a full IT partnership for businesses that don't have a dedicated IT department: connectivity, security hardening, DNS, and where it makes sense, advanced threat detection. The goal is to be the IT team you never had, at a price point that actually makes sense for your business.

What I've actually built

The same system running on my own network.

Everything NetWatch Advanced deploys for clients runs on my own hardware first. The IDS, the DNS stack, the dashboard, the firewall rules: I live with all of the data they produce daily. That's not a sales point, it's just how the development worked. Real network, real traffic, real anomalies.

I hold a B.S. in Cybersecurity with a concentration in Data Analytics, alongside several Google professional certifications in cybersecurity, data analytics, and IT support. The academic foundation matters, but the honest reason I can do this work is that I've already been doing it: iterating on a live system, troubleshooting real problems, and building tools that have to actually work.

What to expect

Your partner, not a vendor.

I am passionate about this work and communicate clearly and honestly. You won't get a sales pitch and you won't get handed off to someone who doesn't know your network. Every client gets direct access to the person who built their system. We speak plainly, skip the acronyms unless they're relevant, and treat every engagement as a partnership from day one. When something goes wrong, reach out. We'll assess the situation, tell you clearly whether it's a network issue, and if it is, we address it. If it isn't, we'll point you toward who can.

In-person only

Every deployment involves an on-site visit. Remote-only setup isn't something we offer: these services require hands-on configuration and a real understanding of your specific environment.

Direct access, always

You're not getting a support ticket system. You get direct contact with the person who built your network. Same person, every time. That's intentional.

No jargon

You don't need to know what a SPAN port is to benefit from one. Everything is explained in plain language, and nothing is done without your understanding and sign-off.

Honest about limitations

NetWatch is a detection system. It doesn't prevent threats. We'll tell you exactly what every service does and doesn't do before any commitment is made.
