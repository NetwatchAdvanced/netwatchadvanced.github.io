What We Do

Services that build on each other.

Every service offering maps to the four-tier engagement model. What's possible at each stage depends on what you're starting with, which is exactly what Tier 1 is for.

Before we begin

Not all hardware supports all features. A standard ISP-provided modem/router cannot do VLAN segmentation, forced DNS routing, or granular firewall rules and those require a more capable router and, in some cases, a managed switch. This is not a problem, but rather useful information. The Tier 1 audit exists to establish exactly what your current hardware supports, what it can't do, and what a hardware upgrade would actually unlock for you.

No service is recommended before that baseline is established. And no hardware purchase is ever required, it's always your call whether the upgrade is worth it. We document what's possible at every level so you can make that decision with complete information and make your decisions based on your goals and the hardware required to achieve them.

Tier 2 Coverage & Infrastructure

◈

Mesh System Integration

Design and deployment of a mesh Wi-Fi system appropriate to your space: node placement, backhaul configuration, and integration with your existing router or gateway. Dead zones eliminated, coverage mapped.

Hardware purchase required if current coverage is insufficient

◈

Firewall Configuration

Firewall rule sets configured for your environment: inbound and outbound traffic controls, default-deny posture, port management, and logging. Configured to what your hardware actually supports.

Logical separation of your network into isolated segments: trusted devices, IoT devices, guest access. Prevents lateral movement if a device is compromised. Requires a VLAN-capable router and managed switch.

Requires hardware capable of 802.1Q — confirmed in Tier 1

◈

VPN Implementation

Secure remote access configuration: either client-to-site VPN for accessing your home network remotely, or site-to-site for connecting multiple locations. Deployed and tested to confirm working.

Remote access configuration included

Hardware Recommendations & Sourcing

Specific, justified hardware recommendations based on your audit findings and goals, for your specific network architecture, never a generic list. We standardize on known, tested equipment so the support surface stays manageable and outcomes are predictable.

Written recommendation provided regardless of whether you proceed with NetWatch or not

◈

Security Architecture Advising

A broader look at your security posture: what's well-configured, what's exposed, and what the realistic threat vectors are for your environment. Plain language, no alarmism, actionable output.

Standalone engagement or included with other Tier 2 work

Tier 3 DNS Layer - no router upgrade required

⬡

Recursive DNS Deployment

Pi-hole with Unbound deployed on a Raspberry Pi you own. Network-wide ad and tracker blocking for all devices using DHCP-assigned DNS, fully recursive resolution with no third-party resolver dependency, and DNSSEC validation. Devices with hardcoded DNS settings can attempt to bypass Pi-hole — full enforcement requires a router capable of NAT port redirection, which is assessed and addressed in Tier 1 and Tier 2.

Works with your existing router, but bypass enforcement depends on router capability

⬡

DNS Analytics & Query Logging

90-day query history, per-device analytics, blocked domain tracking, and health scoring across all devices on your network. Surfaces anomalous DNS behavior: devices querying unusual domains, NXDOMAIN spikes, bypass attempts.

Included with DNS deployment

Open source, no licensing fees. Pi-hole and Unbound are open source software. You're paying for the hardware, the configuration, and the deployment, not a license. The Pi with software is yours permanently.

30-day support included. Every DNS deployment includes 30 days of support coverage: configuration issues, troubleshooting, and break/fix are handled at no additional cost during that window. This covers the most likely incident period and ensures any issues stemming from the deployment itself are resolved without question or cost to you.

After 30 days, support continues on a per-engagement basis. Options at that point are discussed directly but there's no obligation to continue and no automatic charges. There is an option for a monthly retainer to have dedicated IT helpdesk for your network to provide you peace of mind, or a 'banked' approach with a set lump payment made at one time to be used during any networking isues in the future, at a set rate per hour. We strive to offer flexibility to find what works best for you.

Tier 4 NetWatch IDS - full deployment

▣

Suricata IDS Deployment

Suricata intrusion detection system deployed on a dedicated Raspberry Pi, configured for your network, with both signature-based and heuristic detection. Passively monitors all traffic via SPAN port; meaning no inline impact, no performance degradation, and no single point of failure.

Requires managed switch for SPAN port mirroring

▣

NetWatch Dashboard

Purpose-built monitoring dashboard unifying Suricata IDS data and Pi-hole DNS telemetry into a single operational view. Real-time alert feed, geographic traffic intelligence, device inventory and discovery, historical trends, and per-IP investigation with combined DNS and network context.

DNS telemetry requires Tier 3 deployment

▣

Device Inventory & Discovery

Continuous monitoring of your network for new, returning, and unknown devices. Every device that appears on your network is flagged, timestamped, and logged. Named device registry maintained and updated over time.

Included with NetWatch deployment

▣

Geographic Traffic Intelligence

All external connections mapped by country, city, and threat classification. Connection volume, first and last seen timestamps, protocol breakdown, and per-IP investigation which are all resolved locally with no external API dependency.

Included with NetWatch deployment

▣

Anomaly & Behavioral Detection

Beyond signature matching, NetWatch establishes a behavioral baseline for your network and flags deviations: a device making ten times its normal DNS queries, traffic to an unusual country at 3am, a new device appearing where it shouldn't. These events don't match any known signature. They don't need to. Abnormal is enough.

Operates alongside signature detection & covers what signatures can't, to provide full coverage

▣

Alert History & Trend Analysis

A single alert is an event. A pattern of alerts is a story. NetWatch maintains a full historical record of alert activity: recurring triggers from the same source, spikes at unusual hours, slow-burn behavior that only becomes visible over days or weeks. Trend data is what separates a monitoring system from a simple alarm.

Included with NetWatch deployment

Detection, not prevention

NetWatch is an IDS, an intrusion detection system. It sees everything on your network and alerts when something looks wrong. It does not block traffic, intercept connections, or stop attacks in progress.

What it gives you is visibility you currently don't have via a continuous record of network activity, anomaly detection, and alerting when something needs attention. What happens after an alert is security recommendations, to resolve incidents and mitigate future occurrences.

Install Configurations

Hardware setups for Tier 4 deployment.

Two hardware configurations for NetWatch deployment. Both are one-time installs and you own the hardware permanently. The right configuration depends on your network size, existing equipment, and exactly how much visibility and control that you want.

Standard

Visibility

  • Single Raspberry Pi running Suricata IDS, NetWatch dashboard, and Pi-hole + Unbound
  • Suitable for networks seeking visibility, that already have a 'prosumer' grade router deployed
  • Managed switch with SPAN port: enables full passive traffic capture
  • Most affordable entry point to full NetWatch deployment, with some control limitations.

Hardware: Raspberry Pi 5 + managed switch + external storage
One-time hardware purchase and you own everything, forever.

Full

Visibility & Granular Control

  • Everything in Standard, plus router upgrade to hardware supporting VLANs, ACLs, and forced DNS routing
  • IoT device segmentation via VLAN
  • DoH blocking at the router level, meaning no device can bypass DNS filtering
  • Firewall rules configured for your specific environment

Hardware: Standard config + capable router (e.g. TP-Link ER605, 707, etc)
One-time hardware purchase and you own everything, forever.

Ongoing Monitoring

Monthly subscription, after deployment.

NetWatch is a proprietary application, it is not open source software. The monthly subscription covers app access, updates, and ongoing support. Pricing is discussed directly and set per engagement. Tiers 1–2 have no ongoing fees.

NetWatch Standard

Monthly Subscription

NetWatch runs on your hardware, on your network, with no third party data collection nor cloud services utilized. The monthly subscription covers continued app access, software updates, maintenance, and a monthly report covering alert activity, traffic patterns, and any recommended follow-up. This level of support is included for the duration of the subscription.

  • Full NetWatch dashboard access
  • Software updates and maintenance
  • Monthly written report
  • Email support for questions
How it works Hardware and install are a one-time cost. The monthly subscription begins after the initial 2–3 week baseline monitoring period and continues month-to-month with no long-term commitment required.

What happens if you cancel. Your hardware is yours permanently: the Pi, the switch, all of it stays. Suricata is an open-source software, so it will continues running and logging to your device. The NetWatch application itself may, or may NOT continue operating on your hardware, but all support definitively ends immediately on cancellation with no updates, no configuration changes, and no troubleshooting moving forward. Any issues with the application after that point are outside the scope of the service.

Tiers 1–2 are one-time engagements. There are no ongoing fees for auditing, firewall configuration, mesh deployment, or any other service other than DNS protection deployment and the NetWatch application itself.
